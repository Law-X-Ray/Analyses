## Interpretation of Deviations

Observed deviations primarily reflect:

- variation in articulation of interpretive layers
- structural compression of intermediate mechanism links
- terminological variation across procedural concepts
- variation in downstream operational and accountability-related extensions

In most cases, the core procedural and informational mechanism remains preserved despite variation in analytical articulation.

---

## Conclusion

The identified Core Mechanism demonstrates high reproducibility across independent analytical runs and synthesis stages.

Observed variability primarily affects operational articulation, terminology, and downstream extensions rather than the mechanism structure itself.

The strongest convergence appears at the level of stable causal topology linking:

- regulatory complexity
- discretionary procedural control
- informational control
- verification limitation
- selective application

The observed convergence suggests that the reconstructed mechanism is unlikely to result from isolated analytical interpretation or prompt-specific artifacts.
