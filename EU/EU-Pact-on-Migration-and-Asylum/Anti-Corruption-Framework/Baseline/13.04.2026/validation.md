## Validation Approach

The purpose of this section is to assess whether the identified mechanism is observable within the EU Pact on Migration and Asylum framework.

Validation is based on:

- operational screening and procedural routing structures
- publicly available implementation reports and monitoring materials
- supervisory and monitoring procedures
- observable procedural and verification architecture

Validation principle:

Validation focuses on observable procedural, informational, and verification structures rather than on inferred intent, hidden motivation, or isolated implementation outcomes.

## Validation Cases

### Case 1

**Case ID:** Screening and Procedural Classification Structure under the EU Pact  
**Source:** screening procedures and procedural allocation architecture  
**Reference:** EU Pact screening and border procedure framework

### Situation

Authorities operating under the Pact framework are required to conduct screening, registration, procedural classification, and routing of applicants through differentiated procedural tracks.

### Action

Implementing authorities classify applicants and operational situations through internally administered procedural and informational processes.

### Outcome

Initial procedural categorization and operational information generation remain under authority-controlled structures.

### Mechanism Link

regulatory complexity  
→ interpretive asymmetry  
→ discretionary procedural control

### Evidence Level

High

### Interpretation

The procedural architecture operationalizes legal obligations through discretionary classification and routing structures, creating interpretive variability during implementation.

Secondary mechanisms appear as derived and context-dependent effects rather than invariant structural components.
