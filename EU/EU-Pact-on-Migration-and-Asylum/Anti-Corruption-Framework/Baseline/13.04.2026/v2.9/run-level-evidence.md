## Interpretation of Deviations

Observed deviations primarily reflect:

- variation in articulation of implementation and informational layers
- structural compression of intermediate causal links
- terminological variation across procedural and operational concepts
- differing levels of abstraction regarding verification and enforcement structures
- variation in treatment of adaptive and stress-sensitive mechanisms

In earlier runs, several analyses compressed fragmented implementation, informational asymmetry, and verification dependency into broader concepts such as:

- informational control
- interpretive asymmetry
- procedural control

Post-synthesis filtering and verification stages separated these compressed structures into more granular implementation, informational, and verification components.

Behavioral and recursive adaptive mechanisms demonstrated lower structural stability and greater sensitivity to stress-conditioned context assumptions.

As a result, they were excluded from the invariant Core Mechanism and reclassified as secondary or context-sensitive mechanisms.

## Stability Assessment

The strongest cross-run convergence appears at the level of:

- regulatory complexity
- fragmented implementation structures
- frontline discretion
- procedural categorization and routing
- informational asymmetry
- verification dependency
- constrained verification capacity

The informational branch demonstrated particularly strong persistence across synthesis stages, although later filtering replaced stronger “informational control” framing with more structurally bounded concepts such as:

- discretionary data production
- information asymmetry
- verification dependency

This reduced interpretive compression while preserving the underlying procedural and verification architecture.

## Conclusion

The identified Core Mechanism demonstrates high reproducibility across independent analytical runs, synthesis stages, verification review, and structural filtering.

Observed variability primarily affects:

- terminology
- causal granularity
- operational articulation
- adaptive extensions
- downstream amplification layers

The strongest convergence appears at the level of stable causal topology linking:

- regulatory complexity
- fragmented implementation
- frontline procedural discretion
- procedural categorization and routing
- informational asymmetry
- verification dependency
- constrained verification capacity

The observed convergence suggests that the reconstructed mechanism is unlikely to result from isolated analytical interpretation or prompt-specific artifacts.

At the same time, post-verification filtering indicates that some higher-order adaptive and recursive governance interpretations were more context-sensitive and less structurally reproducible than the underlying implementation and verification architecture itself.
