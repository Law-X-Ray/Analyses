- consistency of mechanism reconstruction
- stability of causal links
- preservation of the core informational, reporting, verification, and enforcement structure across runs
- differentiation between invariant structural layers and context-sensitive amplification layers

## Core Mechanism

The following mechanism was reconstructed through cross-run synthesis, structural filtering, reproducibility analysis, and post-verification refinement:

self-declared beneficial ownership reporting  
→ reporting entities control information production  
→ independent verification is limited  
→ non-public and restricted registry architecture limits external scrutiny  
→ distorted or incomplete information becomes difficult to detect  
→ enforcement depends on downstream detection  
→ sanction probability remains limited

Informational branch:

self-generated procedural information  
→ verification dependency  
→ constrained independent observability  
→ persistent informational asymmetry

This mechanism serves as the primary analytical reconstruction used in the system analysis layer.

## Interpretation of Deviations

Observed deviations primarily reflect:

- variation in treatment of post-2025 operational narrowing
- differing emphasis on selective asymmetry and exemption structures
- variation in articulation of informational and verification layers
- structural compression of downstream enforcement logic
- differing levels of abstraction regarding detectability and sanction probability
- variation in treatment of adaptive and recursive amplification mechanisms

In earlier runs, several analyses compressed informational asymmetry, verification dependency, and detectability limitations into broader concepts such as:

- informational control
- verification weakness
- enforcement gap
- constrained accountability

Post-synthesis filtering and verification stages separated these compressed structures into more granular informational, verification, and detectability components.

Behavioral escalation, restructuring arbitrage, and recursive informational degradation mechanisms demonstrated lower structural stability and greater sensitivity to context-conditioned assumptions.

As a result, they were excluded from the final Core Mechanism after structural filtering and reclassified as secondary or context-sensitive mechanisms.

## Stability Assessment

The strongest cross-run convergence appears at the level of:

- self-generated beneficial ownership reporting
- informational control by reporting entities
- limited independent verification
- restricted registry visibility
- constrained detectability
- verification dependency
- informational asymmetry
- downstream enforcement dependence

The informational branch demonstrated particularly strong persistence across synthesis stages, although later filtering replaced broader “informational corruption” framing with more structurally bounded concepts such as:

- self-generated procedural information
- verification dependency
- constrained observability
- informational asymmetry

This reduced interpretive compression while preserving the underlying reporting, verification, and enforcement architecture.

---

## Conclusion

The identified Core Mechanism demonstrates high reproducibility across independent analytical runs, synthesis stages, verification review, and structural filtering.

Observed variability primarily affects:

- terminology
- causal granularity
- treatment of selective asymmetry
- operational articulation
- adaptive extensions
- downstream amplification layers

The strongest convergence appears at the level of stable causal topology linking:

- self-generated reporting information
- informational control
- limited independent verification
- restricted external scrutiny
- constrained detectability
- verification dependency
- downstream enforcement dependence

The observed convergence suggests that the reconstructed mechanism is unlikely to result from isolated analytical interpretation or prompt-specific artifacts.

At the same time, post-verification filtering indicates that some higher-order adaptive and recursive informational degradation interpretations were more context-sensitive and less structurally reproducible than the underlying reporting and verification architecture itself.
