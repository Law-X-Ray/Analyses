## Description

This file presents the structural model of system behavior under regulatory conditions, derived through reproducibility analysis, cross-run structural synthesis, and validation.

The analysis focuses on:

- the core causal mechanism
- secondary mechanisms
- structural configuration

The model identifies structural conditions under which outcomes emerge, including information control, selective disclosure, and verification limitations.

This analysis does not evaluate the law as a whole and does not make normative claims.  
It reconstructs how the system operates at the mechanism level.

---

## Core Mechanism

self-reporting  
→ information control  
→ selective disclosure  
→ verification limitation  
→ detection gap

**Outcome:** constrained detection capacity

**Validation status:** structurally validated

Basis:

- the core mechanism reproduces across independent runs
- the mechanism remains structurally stable across independent synthesis layers
- no major structural contradictions were identified
- behavioral elements are excluded from the core structure

Core exclusions:

- incentive-driven behavior
- interpretative behavior

These elements are treated as secondary or conditional mechanisms rather than components of the invariant structure.

---

### Implication

- detection limitations are structural rather than purely enforcement-driven
- increasing enforcement intensity alone is unlikely to eliminate the constraint

---

### Mechanism Explanation

### self-reporting → information control

The reporting architecture places initial data generation under the control of regulated entities, creating structural control over disclosed information.

### information control → selective disclosure

Control over information creates conditions for variation in disclosure scope and reporting granularity within formal reporting requirements.

### selective disclosure → verification limitation

Incomplete or selectively structured information constrains the scope of independent verification.

### verification limitation → detection gap

Limited verification capacity reduces the probability of identifying inconsistencies or violations.

---

Secondary mechanisms explain variation and amplification effects, but do not determine the core structural outcome.

## Feedback Structure

detection gap  
→ reduced risk of detection  
→ selective disclosure  
→ verification limitation  
→ detection gap

The feedback loop is not required for the core mechanism to operate, but may amplify selective disclosure dynamics under conditions of limited verification.

**Status:** conditionally observable amplification mechanism supported by partial validation evidence.

## Interpretation

The system operates through an informational structure in which:

- information is generated and structurally shaped by regulated actors
- disclosure scope remains variable
- verification depends on available information

As a result, system outcomes are shaped primarily by information control and verification limitations rather than enforcement intensity alone.
