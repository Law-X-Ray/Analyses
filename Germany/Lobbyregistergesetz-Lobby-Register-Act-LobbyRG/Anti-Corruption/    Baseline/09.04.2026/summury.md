## Key Finding

The analysis indicates that limitations of transparency and detection within the lobbying registry system arise primarily from the architecture of information generation and verification rather than from isolated instances of non-compliance.

In particular:

- primary disclosure data is generated by regulated entities themselves
- verification depends on submitted information and remains structurally constrained by it
- detection limitations emerge as a consequence of information architecture rather than solely from weak enforcement capacity

The system reproduces limitations of detection as a structural property of the regulatory architecture rather than merely as a result of insufficient compliance or weak enforcement.

---

## Core Mechanism

self-reporting  
→ information control  
→ selective disclosure  
→ verification limitation  
→ detection gap  

**Result:** reduced system capacity for violation detection.

## Mechanism Structure

### Structural Entry Condition

self-reporting  

A reporting configuration in which regulated entities themselves generate and submit the primary information used by the regulatory system.

---

### STRUCTURAL CORE

information control  
→ verification limitation  
→ detection gap  

---

### Operational Extension

selective disclosure

## Interpretation

The system operates through an information architecture in which disclosure and verification depend on data generated by regulated entities themselves.

Within this structure, self-reporting functions as the entry condition from which information control emerges operationally.
